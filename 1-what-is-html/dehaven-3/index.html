I've played guitar for six years. Here's some songs I never want to hear again.

I've played open mics, local festivals, even a bizzarre gig in a coffee shop the size of an atrium. More often then not, I played classic rock, mostly because it was unoffensive and highly demanded by middle aged white people. Throughout my experience, the following songs brought me the most pain.

  • "Sweet Child O' Mine"

    • This is the only GnR song people know, and if I hear the riff without warning I may burst into tears. I like GnR just fine, but this song is way too overplayed.

  • "Smoke on the Water"

    • I love Deep Purple. I highly recommend Machine Head for whoever wants to get into the world of heavy metal. This song doesn't do the band justice in the least.

  • Stairway to Heaven

    • This song is good, but it's so oversaturated that I can't enjoy it anymore. Same goes with virtually all Zeppelin songs.

  • "Sympathy for the Devil"

    • This one is personal. I despise the Rolling Stones with a passion. Their vanity and problematic songs don't make up for any musicianship they might have. I mean, they labeled themselves the "Greatest Rock Band" while the Beatles were still together. Their heads are very far up their own butts and I think more people should call them out for it.

Conclusion?

Classic rock is by no means bad. There's plenty of really cool stuff buried beneath the overplayed Top 40 hits.
