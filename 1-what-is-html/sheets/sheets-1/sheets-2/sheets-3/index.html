My favorite books of 2021

In no particular order

American Royals

and sequel Majesty

By Katharine McGee

These books were a total treat to read! They were soapy and dramatic and everything you need
when you're going on one year into a pandemic. The premise: What if America had a royal family?
Can't wait for the third book in the series to be released this year!


Fangirl, Vol. 1: The Mange

By Sam Maggs & Rainbow Rowell

Fangirl is still one of my favorite books of all time, and Rainbow Rowell one of my favorite
authors. It's just such a product of it's time-- a very nostalgic time for me now. I was so excited
to learn that it was adapted into a manga series! I got to watch one of my favorite stories come
to life! The art style is so fun, and it's a beautiful adaptation.


Being Lolita

By Alisson Wood

I read this in one day. It was short and utterly devastating. This is a memoir by Wood about
being groomed into a relationship with her high school English teacher, and how she survived.
I was capitvated by Wood's every word; it's one you just can't put down.


The Anthropocene Reviewed

By John Green

John Green's debut work of creative nonfiction involved a series of essays rating seemingly
random aspects of human life on earth. From Kentucky Bluegrass to Penguins of Madagascar to
Halley's Comet, each essay is poignant, witty, and right on the nose of what it means to be
human. John Green made me love the human race again.


People We Meet on Vacation

By Emily Henry

I bought this book to read at the beach, and it was the best beach read I could have asked
for! It's a really well-done, non-cheesy romance (not that I don't also love badly written
cheesy romances). You'll fall in love with Alex and Poppy, and you're gonna get a really
great urge to travel across the world as well.


Any Way the Wind Blows

By Rainbow Rowell

What happens after the chosen one saves the world? Rainbow Rowell explores this in her Simon
Snow series. The first in the series, Carry On, is my favorite book of all time, so to
see this series finally come to a close was bittersweet, to say the least. This series explores
mental health in a really honest way, and it doesn't hold back.


Normal People

By Sally Rooney

This book was recommended to me by countless "booktokers" on TikTok, but as it turns out,
TikTok was right. I finished this in two days in October and haven't stopped thinking about it
since. It's a wonderful exploration of mental health and relationships through the vehicle of
Connell and Marianne, two young adults who always seem to come back to each other throughout
their comings of age.


Heartstopper

Volumes 1-4

By Alice Oseman

This one was read partially through library books and partially on Webtoon. It follows Nick
and Charlie, two English teens who are navigating their relationship among the other troubles
teenage life. I just love a story where it's the couple against the world. It's just nice to
read something where you know the main characters' relationship is solid, and that's exactly
what Oseman does.
