Adi Sircar's Fried Rice Formula

Fried rice is affordable, incredibly versatile, and easy to make.

Meat + egg + vegetable + spice + color + fragrance + flavor + garnishing + rice

I typically use beef, pork, chicken, shrimp, or mixed seafood. Currently, my favorite vegetables are mushrooms and asparagus. I add a couple spoonfuls of Lao Gan Ma's hot chili oil. I get a rich color from soy sauce. I use green onions for garnishing and fragrance. The rice I use is leftover rice from the day before.

I arrange all my ingredients in an organized fashion. Mise en place is critical for safety, accessibility, and great cooking. I always keep raw meats isolated, and I work from left to right. I always keep a clean kitchen. Never trust a dirty chef!

Fried Rice Philosophy

What is fried rice? Fried rice can be explained by observing a spoonful of it. What colors do you see? How does it smell? How does it feel in your mouth? Is there harmony?

Each grain of rice needs color - no whites allowed! Each spoonful of fried rice needs at least four different colors. Meat must be sliced evenly and vegetables must be diced evenly.


When I cook fried rice, I use The Path of the Earth, Moon, and Sun. Earth is the meat and vegetables, Moon is the rice, and Sun is the egg.

  1. Earth

    2. Earth is the meat and vegetables. I add these first. The order matters - different meats and vegetables have different cook times. I add some seasonings and spices at this point.

  2. Moon

    3. Moon is the rice. I use leftover rice. I break apart the clumps in my wok, add soy sauce, hot chili oil, and other seasonings of choice.

  3. Sun

    4. I add the egg last.

After I plate the fried rice, I top it with finely chopped green onions, and serve.
