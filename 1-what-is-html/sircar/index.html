Adi Sircar's Fried Rice Formula

Fried rice is affordable, incredibly versatile, and easy to make.

Meat + egg + vegetable + spice + color + fragrance + flavor + garnishing + rice

I typically use beef, pork, chicken, shrimp, or mixed seafood. Currently, my favorite vegetables are mushrooms and asparagus. I add a couple spoonfuls of Lao Gan Ma's hot chili oil. I get a rich color from soy sauce. I use green onions for garnishing and fragrance. I use leftover rice from yesterday.

I arrange all my ingredients in an organized fashion. Mise en place is critical for safety, accessibility, and great cooking. I always keep raw meats isolated, and I work from left to right. I always keep a clean kitchen. Never trust a dirty chef!

Fried Rice Philosophy

What is fried rice? Fried rice can be explained by observing a spoonful of it. What colors do you see? How does it smell? How does it feel in your mouth? Is there harmony?

Each grain of rice needs color - no whites allowed! Each spoonful of fried rice needs at least four different colors. Meat must be sliced thinly and vegetables must be diced evenly.


When I cook fried rice, I use The Path of the Earth, Moon, and Sun.

  1. Earth

    2. The meat and vegetables are the Earth. I add these first. The order matters - different meats and vegetables have different cook times. I add some seasonings and spices at this point.

  2. Moon

    3. The rice is the Moon. I use leftover rice. I break apart the clumps in my wok, add soy sauce, hot chili oil, and other seasonings of choice.

  3. Sun

    4. The egg is the Sun. I add the eggs last.

After I plate the fried rice, I top it with finely chopped green onions, and serve.
